# Service 2 Calibration: Authoritative Research Foundation

Review date: July 20, 2026

Purpose: define the evidence hierarchy used by the independent AI reviewers and
the calibrated Service 2 rubric.

The target opportunity's captured requirements and application instructions
remain the primary authority. General guidance cannot add an unstated hard
requirement or remove a published one.

## Evidence Hierarchy

1. Captured target requirements and application instructions.
2. Applicable program, public-sector, or sponsor rules.
3. Locale- and document-type guidance.
4. Role-family and employer guidance.
5. General career guidance.
6. Social-media hypotheses, which may create a test but not a scoring rule.

## Material Distinctions

### Document Type

Academic CVs, NIH/NSF biosketches, scholarship CVs, grant profiles, design
portfolios, and conventional employment resumes require different evidence and
structure. Treating all of them as one resume type is a target-type defect.

### Locale

Photo and personal-information conventions conflict across locales. UK and US
federal guidance excludes photos and sensitive demographic details, while
German guidance says photographs remain common in some sectors. The target and
locale control; no universal photo rule is permitted.

### Length

Length is target-specific. Academic CVs may be longer; USAJOBS has a current
two-page rule for covered federal resumes; AfDB's YPP specifies a different
limit. Trakr must not penalize a document solely for violating a generic
one-page slogan.

### Eligibility

Scholarships, fellowships, grants, public-sector roles, and regulated roles
often contain hard gates. A polished document or strong preferred
qualifications cannot average away citizenship, residence, enrollment,
licensing, work-experience, document, or procedural ineligibility.

### Role Evidence

Technical, senior, design, research, and nontechnical targets require different
evidence. Architecture, launched systems, leadership scope, research products,
portfolio reasoning, or stakeholder outcomes cannot be inferred from a shared
keyword or adjacent title.

### ATS

ATS products and employer configurations vary. Trakr may evaluate transparent
document readiness, extractability, headings, terminology, and instruction
compliance. It must not advertise a universal ATS score, pass probability, or
guaranteed format.

## Calibration Rules

- Requirement extraction must preserve required, preferred, instruction, and
  contextual distinctions.
- Applicant evidence must be mapped to the exact requirement it supports.
- Unknown facts remain unknown.
- Inferences receive less confidence than direct evidence.
- Adjacent roles require task-, method-, and output-level equivalence.
- Early-career evidence may include projects, coursework, volunteering,
  research, freelance, and open-source work.
- Seniority requires scope and leadership evidence, not years alone.
- Optimization must preserve claim meaning and uncertainty.
- Accessibility and parseability are separate from candidate qualification.
- Low-consensus locale or professional-practice cases enter the future
  qualified-human-review queue.

## Reviewer Limitation

This research informs AI review and adjudication. It does not create human,
expert, recruiter, scholarship-panel, or grant-panel validation, and it does
not establish hiring-outcome prediction.
